Mission

The Juice Box is a research and intelligence platform dedicated to cataloging, mapping, and analyzing the global landscape of religious organizations. Our goal is to provide a transparent, searchable resource that connects the dots between organizations, the people who lead them, and their worldwide operations.

By aggregating publicly available information and organizing it into a structured, interconnected database, we aim to make religious institutional networks more accessible to researchers, journalists, academics, and the general public.

What We Track

  • Organizations - Religious institutions, congregations, ministries, nonprofits, educational bodies, media outlets, lobbying groups, and umbrella organizations
  • People - Founders, leaders, rabbis, pastors, executives, board members, and key figures affiliated with tracked organizations
  • Connections - Financial ties, leadership overlaps, parent-subsidiary relationships, historical splits, mergers, and partnerships
  • Geography - Country-level breakdown spanning 118 nations across every continent, with organizational presence mapped globally
  • Categories - 34 classification types ranging from Orthodox and Reform denominations to lobbying organizations and media outlets
  • Historical Data - Founding dates, key events, leadership transitions, and organizational evolution over time

Project History

2016 - Project founded. Initial data collection begins with a focus on major organizational networks.
2017 - 2019 - Steady expansion of entries and geographic coverage. Database grows to include dozens of countries.
2020 - 2023 - Platform rebuilt as a modern web application with Node.js and Express. Interactive visualizations added.
2024 - Major data expansion. Entries surpass 800, people database launched, connection mapping added. Deployed to thejuicebox.live.
2025 - Database exceeds 1,100 entries across 118 countries with 950+ tracked individuals. Platform-wide UI refresh with glassmorphism design, Font Awesome iconography, network graph, pathfinder, and comprehensive analytics.

Data Sources and Methodology

All information in The Juice Box is compiled from publicly available sources, including:

  • Official organizational websites and publications
  • Public filings and nonprofit registries (IRS Form 990, Charity Commission, etc.)
  • News reporting from established media outlets
  • Academic research and published studies
  • Government records and public directories
  • Archived web content and historical documents

Each entry is cross-referenced where possible to ensure accuracy. The database is continuously updated as new information becomes available or corrections are identified. We prioritize factual, verifiable information and strive to maintain neutrality in all descriptions.

Technology Stack

  • Backend - Node.js with Express.js
  • Frontend - Vanilla JavaScript, HTML5, CSS3 with glassmorphism design
  • Visualizations - D3.js (network graphs), Chart.js (statistics), Leaflet.js (maps)
  • Icons - Font Awesome 6
  • Hosting - Render.com with custom domain via GoDaddy
  • Data Format - JSON with relational cross-referencing
  • Version Control - Git / GitHub
